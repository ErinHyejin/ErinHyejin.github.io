Pairing and Feedback

Jan 20, 2015

Pair Programming

When I started DevBootcamp course, I did not have sense of belonging that I was a member of 2015 cohort. The only thing that made me to feel I was not alone goint through this journey was having pair programmings.

Overall, pair programming is fun for me. I learn new skills and different ways to solve a problem. In addition, applying what I've learned so far and explaining how I approach a problem to a pair give me a chance to review my knowledge. That also helps me find what is not clear for me.

However, learning and working on the same project remotely are not easy for me, especially as a foreigner. Something frustrating for me is expressing my thoughts and explaining code in English. Though I have a strong concept, sometimes it takes long time for me to express what I want to do clearly. Luckily, all pairs I have met so far were kind, and they communicated well to understand my thoughts.

In addition, it is hard to stick to one role (driver/navigator) during a half of the session because the knowledge level on the specific project is different from pair to pair. Though my role is a navigator for the first half of the session, it is necessary to switch the role in case I don't understand programming directions. In addition, switching roles is somewhat hard if we are so into working on the codes. Of course, switching roles smoothly is necessary for all programmers, so I will practice it more in the next pairing sessions.

Feedback

So far, I received about 10+ feedbacks. I feel good when I get good feedbacks. The members of my cohorts are very kind and communicate with care. Most feedbacks helped me to learn what to improve. Based on the feedbacks, next time, I will drive more than navigate.

Since it is written in English, sometimes I feel it is difficult to write a kind and specific feedback. I don't want to complain to the pair or make any sensitive feedback, but I have hard time to choose right words and right expressions. In addition, since I met good pairs so far, there was less things to give specific, actionable comments for them to improve. I think one peer pairing and one guided pairing session is good for me.
