My Thinking Style

Jan 9, 2014

My Thinking Style

When I took the Thinking Style test, I found my thinking styles are concrete sequential (CS) and abstract sequential (AS). My CS and AS scores were both very high: 42 and 40 each.

The test result indicates that,
- I use my five senses - sight, smell, touch, taste, and hearing (Concrete)
- as well as intuition and imagination (Abstract) when I think.
- In addition, I strongly prefer thinking in an ordered, sequential, and linear way (Sequential).

I always plan ahead because working without plan makes me uncomfortable. I also enjoy dividing big chunk of a task into pieces to avoid working on several project at the same time and feeling overwhelmed. It helps me to achieve small goals for a day and focus on 'now and here' instead of being distracted by other projects.

How Have I been for the Last Three Weeks?

It's been three weeks since I started Dev Bootcamp phase 0 curriculum. I felt overwhemled in the beginning when I saw 10+ challenges in a folder per week with lots of resources to read. Therefore, I reviewed all the challenges in the beginning of each week and set a goal for the day. In this way, instead of being worried about the deadline scanning all the challenges everytime I work, I could focus on the work for the day. I feel excited when I finish the goal at the end of a week.

My Two Big Struggles

Discussions
I understand that discussions and Q&A sessions are helpful to learn something, but I feel less comfortable because of two major reasons:
1. I prefer taking well organized lectures and studying by myself.
2. It is very hard to express my thoughts and opinions in English which is not my first language. When I communicate in pair programming sessions, it was difficult to explain clearly what I want to.

Too much resources to read
In addition, there are so many articles I should read in this curriculum. I always try to check all the suggested materials. If there are many extra sources, I feel overwhemlmed. Because reading in English takes long time and it is hard to get the focal point and remember the contents. I find images, graphs, maps, and charts are more helpful materials for me. I like visual materials which I can see a big picture and flow.

However, I understand any form of materials have their own strength and weakness. Because my learning style is multimodal, I can adjust my learning style according to different learning method. When I train myself to work in random situations, I will do my best to adapt to the situation in my life. I will adjust myself to any kind of learning styles with a growth mindset.
